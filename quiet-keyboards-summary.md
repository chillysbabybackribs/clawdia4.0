# Quiet Keyboard Comparison: Logitech MX Keys S vs. Signature K650

> Research compiled March 2026 | Sources: Logitech.com, RTINGS.com

---

## Overview

Both keyboards are top-rated quiet wireless keyboards from Logitech as of 2026. The **MX Keys S** is the premium pick, while the **Signature K650** is the best budget option, according to RTINGS.

---

## Logitech MX Keys S

**Rating:** #1 Best Quiet Keyboard of 2026 (RTINGS) | ⭐ 4.6/5 (572 reviews, Logitech.com)  
**Price:** $119.99 *(on sale from $129.99)* as of March 2026  
**URL:** https://www.logitech.com/en-us/shop/p/mx-keys-s.920-011559

### Key Facts
- **Switch type:** Scissor switches — quiet with satisfyingly tactile feel
- **Form factor:** Full-size (100%), low-profile flat design
- **Noise:** Extremely quiet keystrokes; solid plastic case minimizes hollow echo and resonance
- **Keycaps:** Spherically-dished to guide fingers to center, reducing mis-keystrokes
- **Backlighting:** Smart adaptive — illuminates when hands approach; auto-adjusts to ambient light
- **Connectivity:** Bluetooth (up to 3 devices simultaneously) or Logi BOLT USB receiver
- **Productivity:** Programmable Smart Actions via Logi Options+ app for macro automation
- **Charging:** USB-C
- **Colors:** Pale Gray, Graphite, Black
- **Sustainability:** Made with recycled plastic

### RTINGS Verdict
> *"Very good for office use"* — exceptionally quiet with tactile feedback that doesn't feel mushy.

---

## Logitech Signature K650

**Rating:** #1 Best Budget Quiet Keyboard of 2026 (RTINGS)  
**Price:** Budget-friendly (sold individually or bundled with Signature M650 mouse)  
**URL:** https://www.rtings.com/keyboard/reviews/logitech/signature-k650  
*(Note: Official Logitech product page returned 404 as of March 2026 — may be discontinued or relocated)*

### Key Facts
- **Switch type:** Rubber dome — among the quietest switch types available, nearly silent in use
- **Form factor:** Full-size (100%) with **integrated wrist rest** and deep-set keys for ergonomic comfort
- **Noise:** Nearly silent; quieter than scissor switches but with less tactile satisfaction
- **Backlighting:** ❌ None — a notable limitation for low-light environments
- **Connectivity:** Logi BOLT USB receiver or Bluetooth *(single device per connection type; no Bluetooth multi-pairing)*
- **OS support:** macOS and Windows (dual key legends)
- **Media keys:** Dedicated row for playback and volume control
- **Build:** Adequate plastic construction; somewhat flimsy compared to MX Keys S

### RTINGS Verdict
> *"Decent office keyboard"* — rubber domes are nearly silent but feel mushy; best for users prioritizing silence and budget over tactile feel.

## Recommendation

- **Choose the MX Keys S** if you want premium build quality, multi-device Bluetooth, backlighting, and satisfying tactile feedback at a higher price point.
- **Choose the Signature K650** if you're on a budget, want maximum silence, or prefer an integrated wrist rest for all-day typing comfort.
